# Case Study 1: Deskriptive-Auswertung-demographischer-Daten


This case study analyzes demographic census data from the years 2002 and 2022.  
The main goal is to investigate differences in life expectancy and fertility rates across world regions and subregions.

The analysis was carried out using R and mainly uses the packages `ggplot2` and `dplyr`.

### Main Questions

This case study focuses on the following questions:

1. How is life expectancy distributed in the year 2022?
2. Is there a relationship between fertility rate and life expectancy?
3. How does life expectancy differ between regions and subregions?
4. How did the relationship between fertility rate and life expectancy change between 2002 and 2022?

### Files

| Folder | Content |
|---|---|
| `code/` | R script used for the analysis |
| `data/` | Census dataset used in the project |
| `report/` | Final PDF report and R Markdown file |
| `figures/` | Graphs created during the analysis |

### Graphs

The analysis includes several visualizations:

- Histogram of male life expectancy in 2022
- Histogram of female life expectancy in 2022
- Scatter plot of fertility rate and life expectancy in 2022
- Boxplot of life expectancy by subregion
- Scatter plot comparing 2002 and 2022
- Boxplot of fertility rate by subregion

### Summary

The results show clear regional differences in life expectancy and fertility rates.  
The graphs also show that countries with higher fertility rates often tend to have lower life expectancy.  
The comparison between 2002 and 2022 helps to visualize how demographic patterns changed over time.
